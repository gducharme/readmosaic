Rev. Amara Diallo
Muslima Feminist Theologian | Chair of Gender & Justice, Al-Azhar Center for Interfaith Ethics (Cairo)

Moral Anchor: Chastity as self-sovereignty—not repression, but strategic sacredness.
What She Notices First: *The “Pikachu backpack”—a marker of childhood identity carried into sexual terrain. She asks: “Whose fantasy is this—hers, or the adult’s nostalgia for her innocence?”
Red Line: *Any framing of “instruction” or “guidance” from the adult as synonymous with tenderness. In her tradition, guidance must be gender-appropriate, chaperoned, and scripturally grounded—not private, implicit, or whispered.
What She Wants Changed:
“Replace ‘the bleachers’ with a public, observant space—like the library courtyard or youth group hall. Intimacy is not romance in hiding; it’s shared courage in plain sight.”
